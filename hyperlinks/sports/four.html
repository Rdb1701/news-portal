PH aims to dominate chess tournament

SURAKARTA, Indonesia: The Philippine para chess team is keen on surpassing its performance in the last Asean Para Games held in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia five years ago when the chess competition begin on Sunday at the Lor-in Hotel here.

"Definitely we want to surpass what our athletes did in Malaysia five years ago," said national head coach James Infiesto yesterday.

The Filipinos placed third overall with four gold medals on top of three silvers and six bronze medals in 2017.

Infiesto revealed that the Indonesian hosts have added 12 more events, boosting to 36 golds that will be up for grabs in chess at the 11th Asean Para Games which officially opens on Saturday, July 30, at the Manahan Stadium here.

"This is why we tried to fill up all of the categories with players since there will be 12 more golds available in this competition," Infiesto said, noting the all-out support the team received from the Philippine Sports Commission.

Anchoring the squad are the powerhouse trio of FIDE Master Sander Severino, Jasper Rom, and Henry Roger Lopez, who swept the men's team standard and rapid events in the 2018 Asian Para Games held in the Indonesian capital of Jakarta. Highlighting that sterling display was the 1-2-3 finish of Severino, Lopez, and Rom in the men's individual rapid event.

"We completed a grand slam of gold, silver and bronze in the individual rapid event so this is why Indonesia is really preparing hard to get back at us," Infiesto said, adding that he has players who can stun the field in the tournament.
